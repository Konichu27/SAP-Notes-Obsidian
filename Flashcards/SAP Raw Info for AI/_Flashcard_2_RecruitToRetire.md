In detail, the SAP Success Factors HCM Suite includes the following solutions:

- **Onboarding** provides the tools for engaging with external and internal new hires to quickly help them become productive members of the organization.
- **Learning:** SAP SuccessFactors Learning is a Learning Management System (LMS) that allows users to be assigned, to self-assign, and complete learning items, such as courses and other learning activities.
- **Succession** enables organizations to retain their talent, identify talent gaps, and create succession plans by nominating employees to positions aligned with expertise and career aspirations.
- **Compensation** helps organizations build and manage strategic compensation programs that align with the needs of their employees, business, and budget.
- **Performance and Goals:** SAP SuccessFactors Goal Management enables employees and managers to align individual goals with business objectives. It helps organizations ensure that all employees are aligned and work on the things that matter most. Using SAP SuccessFactors Performance Management, employees and managers work together to assess employees performance, including their goal achievements and competencies.
- **Career Development :** SAP SuccessFactors Career Development Planning (CDP) enables employees to target future roles and identify competency gaps to evaluate their readiness for the next steps in their careers.
- **People Analytics** is the overall solution for all reporting in SAP SuccessFactors. It allows users to build interactive dashboards and tiles, which enable managers to view a summary of information for their teams.
- **Employee Central** provides a reliable, consistent system of record. It makes tasks less manual and gives employees the tools needed to automate time-consuming tasks.
- **Recruiting** helps companies source, engage, and hire the best talent. It uniquely combines a powerful Applicant Tracking System (ATS), job distribution, responsive career site, candidate relationship management, and line-of-site analytics into a single solution.

## The Recruiting Process

The following graphic shows the various steps within the recruiting process.

![Process diagram of the hiring lifecycle that progresses from Job Requisition to a Successful Candidate. The recruiter's job starts with creating a job requisition and ends when a successful candidate has been offered the job.

1. What role needs to be filled? The creation of the job requisition begins the recruiting process. During this step, the job requisition form is used to capture all the necessary information about the job, including the following:
    
    - Job title
    - Job description
    - Location
    - Hiring manager
    - Compensation data
    - Other required details used for the job posting
    
    Using the _route map_, the job requisition is sent for approval before it can be posted and advertised through various channels.
    
2. Where will the job be posted and advertised? After the job requisition is approved, the recruiter can choose how to advertise the position.
    - It can be posted to both the internal and external career sites, recruiting agencies, and to various job boards using _recruiting posting_.
    - Private postings allow for confidential positions to be sent directly to candidates discreetly, without a public posting.
3. How will candidate data be collected? Using a combination of the Candidate Profile, Application, and Pre-screening questions, candidate data can be strategically collected to help with the sourcing, screening. and selection process.
    - The _candidate profile_ is used to create a candidate's account, allowing for their basic information to be stored. Resume parsing and LinkedIn integrations allow for quicker completion of the candidate profile. Candidate profile information is available for recruiters to search through in the candidate database.
    - The _application_ collects the data necessary to screen candidates. The application may include qualification questions related to the position, diversity questions necessary for local compliance, and data for reporting.
    - The _pre-screening questions_ are role-specific qualification questions that can be used as disqualifiers (if a candidate answers incorrectly, they are automatically disqualified) or to give an application an overall score or grade
4. What happens after the candidate applies?
    
    Once a candidate applies, recruiters can manage candidate applications using the _talent pipeline_ in the _candidate workbench_.
    
    The talent pipeline is configured to reflect the recruiting organization's hiring process. As a candidate progresses through the recruiting process, the recruiters can move the candidates through the Talent Pipeline accordingly.
    
    To aid with more complex processes, certain application features, such as interview scheduling, interview assessments, background check and assessment integrations are available to ensure the appropriate screening data is collected and documented on the candidate's application.
    
5. What is the job offer process? Once a candidate is selected for hire, recruiters can trigger the offer process by using the offer details to capture all job offer and compensation details.
    
    Once the job offer has been approved, an offer letter can be created and sent to the candidate via the following channels:
    
    - Verbally
    - E-mail (text or PDF attachment)
    - Online via the Career Site (with option to require electronic candidate signature)

## Initiating Onboarding of New Hires

The onboarding process starts with initiating onboarding and ends with the new recruit getting converted into an employee (internal user).

Onboarding can either be initiated through an Applicant Tracking System (ATS), such as SAP SuccessFactors Recruiting or initiated manually.

## High Level Onboarding Steps

Once the onboarding process for a new hire is initiated, the hiring manager and new hire are assigned to different onboarding tasks that need to be completed in a timely manner. Other departments or teams can also be involved in the onboarding process to make sure that the new hires have everything they need before their first day at work.

The following picture provides a high level overview of the onboarding steps.

![The image illustrates the onboarding process flow between a new hire and a hiring manager, highlighting key steps: (1) an email notification is sent to start onboarding, (2) tasks are assigned to the new hire, (3) documents are reviewed and signed, (4) the hiring manager adds hiring data, and (5) the new hire's status is activated.](https://learning.sap.com/service/media/topic/e814ed39-8e0a-4d9d-8513-fc8c919908cd/IEE2E_2508_en-US_media/IEE2E_2508_en-US_images/Onboarding%20Steps.png "The image illustrates the onboarding process flow between a new hire and a hiring manager, highlighting key steps: (1) an email notification is sent to start onboarding, (2) tasks are assigned to the new hire, (3) documents are reviewed and signed, (4) the hiring manager adds hiring data, and (5) the new hire's status is activated.")

1. The hiring manager, receives an e-mail notification that the onboarding process for their new hire has begun. They review the data related to the new hire and assign tasks to the new hire to provide additional information.
2. The new hire, receives an e-mail containing the login URL and credentials to access SAP SuccessFactors. They complete the assigned tasks by using the _For You Today_ section of the home page.
3. The SAP SucccessFactors Onboarding system generates the required documents. The system notifies both, the hiring manager and new hire, that they must review and sign these documents.
4. Hiring Manager/HR Admin: Once the documents are signed, the new hire is listed under _Manage Pending Hires_ tool. The hiring manager or HR Admin then adds the hiring data of the new hire in SAP SuccessFactors Employee Central.
5. On the new hire's start date, SAP SuccessFactors activates their status and employment information.

You will see now how a new hire completes the required data collection and signs the required documents. You will also learn how a hiring manager or HR representative completes the creation of the employee profile of the new hire in the Human Resource Information System (HRIS). In our example, the HRIS is SAP SuccessFactors Employee Central.

## SAP SuccessFactors Employee Central Core Structure

SAP SuccessFactors Employee Central provides a reliable, consistent system of record for global companies and helps enhance the overall experience for highly diverse, mobile employees. It makes tasks less manual for employees, managers, and HR, and gives them the tools needed to automate time-consuming tasks.

The following graphic provides an overview of the Employee Central (EC) core structure.

![The figure illustrates the structure of an HR management system, highlighting key components such as workflows, role-based permissions, and self-service functionality for managers and employees. Using self-service tools, companies can empower employees, managers, and HR to manage employee records by initiating transactions directly in the _People Profile_ interface. _People Profile_ is the unified page where employees can view their employee record.

1. **Workflows, Event Reasons and Business Rules** are used in transactions in EC:
    - The workflow defines the approval process.
    - Event reasons are used to set the employee status for employment change and defines the reason an event has taken place.
    - The business rules add application logic to transactions.
2. **Self Service:** One of the key features of SAP SuccessFactors Employee Central (EC) is the ease with which you can maintain data. EC offers both employee and manager self-service.
3. **Role-Based Permissions** makes it possible to grant different levels of read or write access, depending on the role of the user (HR, Employees, Managers, etc.).
4. **HR data** includes employee personal data (such as date of birth and national ID) and employment data (such as job title and compensation).
5. **Foundation Objects** are a fundamental aspect of SAP SuccessFactors Employee Central. This allows the company to define and store its organization, pay, and job structures, which helps organize employee information.
