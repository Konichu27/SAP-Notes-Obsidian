# Sales Cycle
- Goes from **Lead -> Opportunity -> Quotation -> Sales Order**
- Each of these are a separate **document**.

![[Pasted image 20260302204103.png]]

### Document Flow
- The king of SAP Sales Cloud
- Every activity is **linked together** in one GUI.
## Lead to Opportunity
- Can be either **B2C** or **B2B**
- Can be either **referral** or from a response from a **marketing campaign**

An interface is assigned to facilitate the lead-to-opportunity step.

![[Pasted image 20260302204529.png]]

## Opportunity to Quote
- **Opportunity** can be defined as a *sales document* with the following data:
	- **Customer**, also known as a *sales prospect*
	- **Requested Products & Services**
	- **Total Volume**
	- **Expected Sales Volume**
	- **Estimated Sales Probability
- **SAP Buying Center** can be used to manage the partners in an opportunity. Whether contact-to-contact or contact-to-employee
	- [Work with Buying Center | SAP Help Portal](https://help.sap.com/docs/sap-cloud-for-customer/solution-guide-for-sap-sales-cloud/work-with-buying-center)
- **Guided selling** is also an option; it allows the sales team to be guided in creating quotations & sales orders.
