## Asset Accounting
- **Asset** - anything a business owns that has value
- Asset accounting is also important for design-to-operate.
## Types of Assets
- **Intangible assets** - ex. patents, software
- **Fixed assets** - ex. property, machines, down payments on other fixed assets
- **Financial assets** - ex. investments, securities

FI-AA in SAP S/4HANA focuses on *fixed assets*.
Financial assets are under [SAP Treasury and Risk Management](https://www.sap.com/products/financial-management/treasury-risk-management.html) and will not be covered in this module.
## Asset History Sheet
- SAP-specific, not a general accounting thing
- Lists down the balances and their changes for fixed assets
	- Acquisitions, retirements, transfers, depreciation, etc.
- Also known as the **notes to the financial statements** (remember this!)

## Life-Cycle of a Fixed Asset
![[Pasted image 20260220180423.png]]

1. **Create asset master record**
	- Each asset has an *asset master record*.
	- **Asset master record** - consists of the following aspects:
	- **General master data** - describes *concrete information* about the fixed asset
		- Description, account assignments (ex. to cost center), postings, physical inventory, asset origin, etc.
	- **Asset value data** - describes the *valuation* of the asset
		- Depreciation keys, useful life, expired useful life, starting date of depreciation, scrap value
2. **Asset acquisitions**
	- Depending on the functional module, there are different roles of asset acquisitions:
	- **Asset Accounting (FI-AA)** - Either with or without Accounts Payable integration.
	- **Procurement** - Assets are posted and activated in Asset Accounting during the *Goods Receipt – Invoice Receipt* process
3. **Depreciations**
	- Part of the *asset life cycle*
	- **Ordinary depreciation** - planned depreciation, from normal use 
	- **Unplanned depreciation** - unusual influences, represents permanent decrease in valuation
	- **Special depreciation** - when it happens due to tax laws
4. **Asset retirement**
	- When an asset is no longer used by the company
	- **Retirement by sale** - when an asset is sold for example.
		- Can have Accounts Receivable integration, thru a clearing account
	- **Retirement by scrapping** - no revenue is generated
		- Instead of a revenue/loss posting (P&L account), the posting "_Loss made on asset retirement without revenue_" is made

## Elements of a Fixed Asset Master Record
![[Pasted image 20260220190005.png]]

> [!NOTE] How keys work
> You may notice that the word "key" is being thrown around, ex "depreciation key" or "account determination key".



1. **Asset class** - refers to the type of asset
	- Each asset class has a 4-digit code. For example, 1000 for real estate (land), 1100 for buildings, etc.
	- **Account determination key** - lists down *all* the G/L accounts required for the asset
		- Ex. Acquisition costs, manufacturing costs, expense accounts (for depreciation)
	- **Control parameters and default values** - for depreciation calculation
	- **Asset number**
	- **Master data layout**
2. **Depreciation areas** - the areas calculated for depreciation, depending on valuation purpose or regional tax code
	- Each depreciation area is seen in the form of a **valuation view** in Asset Accounting

## Depreciation Calculation

> $$\text{Depreciation} = \frac{\text{Acquisition Cost}}{\text{Expected Useful Life}}$$
> Example:
> $$\displaylines{\text{Acquisition Costs} =  \text{€ }10,000 \\ \text{Useful life} = \text{5 years} \\ \text{Depreciation} = \frac{\text{€ } 10,000}{\text{5 years}} = \text{€ 2,000 per year}}$$
