## Planning Cycle
![[Pasted image 20260220204402.png]]
- The overall *meta-planning process* (i.e., the plan for plans)
- The above is an example of a typical planning process. Note that this can differ per company

1. **Sales Planning** - Bike Company DE wants to sell 10,000 e-bikes (model I-F3000) in EMEA
2. **Sales and Operations Planning** - The production demands are defined (ex. 2000 bikes are already in stock so only 8000 are needed to be made)
3. **Production Planning** - plan orders are created according to SOP's demand
	- Activity Input Quantities - the machine and labor capacities required. Ex:
		- 2,700 Personnel Hours - Production
		- 1,300 Machine Hours – Production
- **Cost Center and Activity Type** - transfer of cost accounting of activities from other departments or cost centers. Ex. personnel planning from SAP Human Experience Management or planned depreciations from Asset Accounting.
	- Ex. (simplified):
	    - Total costs of personnel in the Production cost center: € 1,000,000 / period
	    - Total activity of the Production cost center in hours: 20,000 H / period
	    - Price for the Personnel Hours Activity Type: € 50/Hour
- **Material Cost Estimate** - cost of products
	- Material Costs + Production Costs = **Cost of Goods Manufactured**
	- Material Costs + Production Costs + Sales Costs and Administrative Overhead Costs = **Cost of Goods Sold**
- **Profit Planning** - Calculations of revenues, sales deductions, costs, and profits based on the planned sales quantities.

> [!NOTE]
> For each step, a detailed plan/report is bumped into the next step
