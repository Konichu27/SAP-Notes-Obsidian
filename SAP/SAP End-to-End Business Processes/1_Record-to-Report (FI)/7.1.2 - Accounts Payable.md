## Business Partner
- The **center** of the vendor master data in SAP S/4 HANA
- Involves *general data* and *financial accounting data*
	- Recall: [[1.6 - Managing Accounts Payable#Types of Data]]
	- *General data* is vendor name, address, contacts, etc. for a single client.
	- *Financial accounting data* is company code-related data such as G/L links, correspondence, payment methods, etc.

The following info is especially important when creating a new business partner.
### Business Partner Roles
- All business partners must have a predefined **role**.
	- Examples of roles include *Customer*, *Supplier*, *FI Customer*, and *FI Vendor*.
### Business Partner Assignment
- **Assignment**
	- Each business partner must be assigned to a *grouping*. This grouping determines the business partner's *number*.
	- This number can be assigned externally (manually) or internally (based on grouping)
		- Ex. 10101018, from company code 1010
- **Correspondence language**
	- Each business partner can be assigned a *standard corresponding language*, which is the language for the document used
- **Reconciliation Account**
	- **K (Vendors)** - defines G/L reconciliation accounts
		- Either **D (Customers)** for *Accounts Receivable*, or **A (Assets)** for *Asset Accounting*

## Vendor Invoice Process

![[Pasted image 20260219185722.png]]

Assume a new invoice for €1,000 for office supplies (e.g., printer cartridges), paid by Bike Company DE. The vendor is **T-BP00**.

It is automatically posted in **3** places:

1. The **Accounts Payable** subledger, under **Vendor T-BP00**.
	- Each transaction in the subledger is assigned a **status**. This is called **open item management**, and declares if the item is paid or not.
2. The **G/L reconciliation account** (21000000* Payables Domestic).
	- You **cannot post directly** to a reconciliation account. It is only updated in conjunction with the vendor transaction posting.
3. The **P&L expense account** (65100000 Office Supplies).
	- In this case, since the account is a **primary cost** (CO), it *must* be assigned to a cost object. In this case, it's Cost Center 10101801.
